poster designs

This mock poster series was done as a project for my fundamentals design class.

The concept behind this poster series is utilizing letter shape to create a centralized illustration or feeling that conveys the artist's musical stylings.

Swear and Shake is an indie folk band, giving it down-to-earth, nature-like qualities. Thus, the coloring followed that theme with the illustration of the S's lending way to ideas of comraderie and home.

Pretty Lights is a electronic dj with warm undertones to his songs. There's a sense of being surrounded by acquaintances in a lounge in the 50s. Thus, the colors are overwhelmingly warm. The illustration of the P as the sun further drives the idea of warmth.

The Knocks is an electronic duo with a very groovy sound. They seem, to me, to be an artist that you listen to surrounded by close friends, swaying and dancing. Thus, the colors have a warmth to them, but with a retro styling. The illustrations of the T and K are very straightforward, like the band, lanky and awkward but still cool.

drawbridge internship

My role as a front-end software engineering intern at Drawbridge, a startup based in San Mateo, was to assist the front-end engineering department on various features and bug fixes each spring cycle. Unlike other internships where an intern is given a summer project for the entirety of the internship, I was treated as a full-time employee, implementing features and working with the engineering, marketing, and sales team to complete tasks for each sprint.

Due to the relatively small nature of Drawbridge, the front-end engineering team consisted of two other full-time employees and myself. This gave me an amazing opportunity to gain experience in a variety of technologies as well as made me feel personally responsible and accountable for all the code I contributed to the codebase.

I worked extensively with Javascript, PHP, and some Java during my time at drawbridge. I also gained further experience in frameworks such as base.js, d3, jQuery, underscore, etc.

zwp student consulting

I had a great experience taking the role of lead software engineer and student consultant to Zero Waste Pittsburgh's intiative to try to bring the nonprofit into the technical era. I worked in a team of three to help aid Zero Waste Pittsburgh into taking the first steps of integrating technology into their current workflow.

Our main point of contact was Kyle Winkler, the director of the program. With his help, we were able to understand the needs of the nonprofit and create an application to lessen the burden of the initial waste audit process, a free service in which Kyle performs a cursory waste audit to a potential customer.

The final deliverable reduced turnaround time and wasted energy on this free initial 'sales pitch' as well as gave Kyle a centralized location to keep all future data.

Technologies used in this project included Ruby on Rails, Paperclip, WickedPDF, etc.

apollo

Below is a screenshot for the homepage of Apollo:

Apollo is deployed on heroku.

Apollo is a web application (written in Javascript), which allows users to discover new artists through searching artists they love.

Apollo uses last.fm and 7digital API in order to find similar artists. Once a list is populated, the user can click through and find a suitable artist to learn more about. By spotlighting the artist, the user is provided with a bio, top tracks, and albums available for purchase on 7digital.

Apollo is still very much in its developmental stages. There are some UI bugs that we'd like to fix as well as features we hope to add.

human-computer interaction institute

research life at carnegie mellon:

The Social Computing group of the Human-Computer Interaction Institute is focused on social computing and how the way we interact through technology has changed social norms.

I first joined the Social Computing group as an Independent Study student working with Robert Kraut and Tatiana Vlahovic, designing a study that deals with how people interact on Facebook. I learned quite a bit about experimental design, questionnaire wording and content, experimental flow, etc. as well as contributed to more technical aspects of the study such as building an application using PHP, mySQL, Facebook API, etc. The study is now ongoing and my status of that of research assistant to the social computing group.

apex

buggy life at carnegie mellon:

As one of the first members of Apex, I learned a lot about Carnegie Mellon culture through the organization. Buggy is a something all to Carnegie Mellon itself. The idea is to fit a driver (my role) into a small vertical vehicle that is pushed by pushers, who are runners keeping the buggy moving along during the uphills. By joining Apex, I was able to understand the struggles of being an organization at its infancy. The experience has been invaluable to my college years and has been the avenue where I've met and connected with the most friendly, genuine, passionate people I've ever had the chance to meet. It contributed greatly to an amazing freshman experience.

infant language learning lab

research life at carnegie mellon:

The Infant Language Learning Lab is run by Professor Erik Thiessen. The lab conducts studies on infants as well as adults to find out how infants learn language and what affects an infant's ability to learn. A great deal of the studies involve statistical learning. As a research assistant at the lab, I learned how to run studies, interact with infants, and how to conduct myself in a lab.

I had joined the lab as a freshman still unsure of the path I wanted to take in my professional career. Before realizing my passion was in web development, I had seriously thought about going to medical school. Thus, the lab provided a great opportunity to get real experience dealing with the most difficult patients: infants. As someone who loves children, working at the lab was incredibly enjoyable and I gained valuable insight on how research works in academia.
